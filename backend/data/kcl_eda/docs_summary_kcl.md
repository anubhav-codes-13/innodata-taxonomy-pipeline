## 1. What the data actually is

Same `<KEA-publ>` wrapper as KA, but **five** inner document types (one more than KA — `<caselaw>` is new):

| Inner tag | Count | What it holds | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| `<essay>` | 13 | *Competition Law in India* book chapters, Merger Thresholds Monitor country reports, CLWE monograph | `KLI-KCL-Roy-2024-Ch01.xml`, `KLI-KCL-MTM-Canada.xml` |
| `<legislation>` | 3 | EU Commission Notices / Communications | `KLI-KCL-SMEU-2406011.xml` |
| `<caselaw>` | 2 | **NEW**: EU court decisions with full case metadata | `KLI-KCL-0949015.xml`, `KLI-KCL-CL-CompL200500292.xml` |
| `<commentary>` | 2 | Common Market Law Review articles (legis-comment variant) | `KLI-KCL-COLA-610503.xml` |
| `<booktoc>` | 1 | TOC pointing at the Roy 2024 book chapters | `TOC-Roy-2024.xml` |

Everything is **English** (`lang="en"`) and every doc carries ISBN or ISSN. The parser handles all five types uniformly now — `<caselaw>` was added specifically for this repository.

### Publisher / container
KCL is narrower and more centred than KA — effectively **5 containers**, one dominant:
- **12 docs** from *Competition Law in India: A Practical Guide (Second Edition)* — the Roy 2024 book (6 chapters + 4 annexes + book front-matter + TOC)
- 4 *Selected Materials from the European Union* (EU legislation/case snippets)
- 2 *Common Market Law Review* articles
- 2 *Merger Thresholds Monitor* country reports (Bolivia, Canada)
- 1 *Competition Law in Western Europe and the USA* (1976 — very old)

### Product-tagging (`<cust-group>`)
- `(KCL,)` — 19 docs
- `(KCL, CH)` — 2 docs (the CMLR articles)

Narrower than KA (which also had IHB). One product cluster dominates.

### Publication timeline
`1976: 1, 2024: 14, 2025: 2` — heavily 2024-weighted, driven by the Roy book. One anomalous 1976 entry (`KLI-KCL-CL-CompL200500292` — old Coca-Cola CFI case).

### Subject matter
**EU / competition law**: mergers, antitrust, cartels, state aid. Completely different substantive domain from KA (international arbitration).

## 3. Existing vocabularies (seed for Requirement #3)

### Topics — **a real taxonomy, not noise** (unlike KA)

12 docs carry 20 topic annotations across just **5 distinct values**:

| Topic | Occurrences |
|---|---|
| Mergers | 8 |
| Antitrust | 7 |
| Cartels | 2 |
| Liberalisation | 1 |
| State Aid | 1 |

This is a **clean, controlled vocabulary** — no casing variants, no inflection duplicates, and every value is a recognised competition-law sub-domain. Topics also carry `id` attributes like `KCL-004` → there is an underlying **coded topic hierarchy** already maintained somewhere in the KCL editorial system that we do not have direct access to but can reconstruct by collecting all unique `(id, text)` pairs.

**Action:** harvest the full `(topic-id, topic-text)` pairs from the full KCL corpus → that *is* the seed taxonomy for Req #3. Extend this taxonomy to KA by zero-shot classification.

### Keywords — sparse and narrow
Only 6 unique keywords (from 1 caselaw doc):
- Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations
- Directive 2003/55/EC
- Internal market in natural gas
- Definitive designation of system operators
- Decision exempting major new gas infrastructures…
- Publication, consultation and notification obligations

These read like **automatically-generated case-law index terms** (verbose, ECJ-headnote style), not editorially-curated author keywords. Different provenance from KA's keywords (which are concise author-chosen terms like "state immunity", "Generative AI").

**Implication for Req #1/#3:** don't blindly merge KA and KCL keyword vocabularies — they are different species of annotation. Normalise and segment (origin = "author" vs "headnote") before using.

### Organizations — absent
Zero `<organization>` tags across all 21 files. At full corpus scale this is probably also sparse. **Must be filled by NER** if it is to be a usable facet.

### Countries
38% populated — notably from:
- MTM reports (Bolivia, Canada) — fully country-scoped
- Roy chapters — all 6 chapters tagged `India`

So country is already reliable when editorially appropriate; no gap to fill.

## 4. `<caselaw>` — the structural find

**This doc type is not in KA.** It is the richest enrichment schema in the entire Kluwer stack:

```
<caselaw id="..." lang="en">
  <frontmatter/>                       ← title/publisher/ISBN as usual
  <juris juris-type="court-decision">
    <juris-description>
      <topic id="KCL-004">Liberalisation</topic>   ← ID-coded taxonomy
      <juris-authority>… European Union Court of Justice …</juris-authority>
      <juris-name/> <juris-number>C-475/08</juris-number>
      <juris-date value="20091203"/>
      <juris-parties>
        <juris-party>Applicant Commission</juris-party>
        <juris-party>Defendant Belgium</juris-party>
      </juris-parties>
      <keywords>…</keywords>
      <references/>                    ← inter-doc link slot
    </juris-description>
    <juris-text>
      <section id="a00001">…</section>
      …
    </juris-text>
  </juris>
</caselaw>
```

Every caselaw doc exposes: court, case number, date, typed parties (Applicant/Defendant/Intervener), topic-with-id, keywords, references, sectioned body. **This is precisely the target schema for Req #4 (ingesting primary content — awards, case decisions).** The ingestion pipeline should emit `<caselaw>` + `<juris-description>` and your NLP stack just has to fill in topic/keywords/references.

The parser now surfaces all of this (`Document.juris_parties`, `juris_court`, `juris_date`, `juris_case_name`, `juris_case_number`) so downstream code can treat caselaw uniformly with commentary case-notes.

Two distinct populations visible:

## 6. Cross-reference graph

- **15 internal edges** (3× smaller than KA's 51 from the same parser).
- **Edge types:** `toc` (10, TOC→chapter — all resolve), `ref` (5 — these are mostly xrefs to PDF companion files, not other KCL docs).
- **10/15 edges resolve** within the sample corpus — much higher resolution rate than KA (5/51) because the Roy book has a clean TOC→chapter structure.

Observations:
- KCL body text uses much less inline cross-referencing than KA (which had 37 `ref`-typed footnote citations). Partly because book chapters tend to cite outside the corpus; partly because less editorial cross-linking has been done.
- The TOC→chapter structure is a **reliable "within-work" spine**. Recommend treating it as one tier of the relationship graph (hierarchy) separate from the citation graph (horizontal).

## 7. KCL vs KA — what matters for a unified pipeline

| Aspect | KA (arbitration) | KCL (competition) | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Sample size | 23 files | 21 files | comparable |
| Dominant doc types | essay + legislation | essay + legislation + **caselaw** | caselaw-handling is KCL-specific |
| Topic coverage | 4% | **57%** | KCL can train/seed a topic classifier for KA |
| Keyword coverage | **22%** | 5% | KA seeds keyword extraction patterns for KCL |
| Topic vocab quality | 1 noisy entry | **5 clean canonical entries** | KCL taxonomy is production-ready; KA needs one |
| Organization tags | 8.7% | 0% | both need NER enrichment |
| Country tags | 22% | 38% | both editorially reliable where present |
| Xref density | high (2.2 /doc) | low (0.7 /doc) | KA graph denser; KCL relies on TOC hierarchy |
| Structural schema fit for ingestion | juris-comment (commentary) | **juris + juris-description (caselaw)** | Req #4 target = KCL caselaw schema |
| Median doc size | 12k words | 7k words | KCL more bimodal |
| Sub-product (`cust-group`) | KA + IHB/CH | KCL + CH only | narrower than KA |
| Languages | English only | English only | (both samples; full corpora likely wider) |

### What this means for each requirement

**Req #1 — Blog → paid-content bridging**
KCL already has the topic layer needed to match blog topics against competition-law content. KA does not. Start blog-bridging with **KCL as the proof-of-concept** (high signal) and use it to validate the topic-match ranking logic before extending to KA.

**Req #2 — Enrich content with new tags**
Two distinct gaps to fill, not one:
- For KA: build a topic classifier (seed it with KCL's taxonomy patterns + arbitration-specific concepts).
- For KCL: build a keyword extractor (seed it with KA's author-keyword style).
- Both: NER for `<organization>` — zero coverage in KCL, low in KA.

**Req #3 — Topic/keyword dictionary with relationships**
KCL's `<topic id="KCL-XXX">` codes strongly suggest an existing editorial taxonomy. **Before building anything, ask for the controlled topic list** — it may already exist internally. Then:
1. Nodes: harvest (topic-id, topic-text, cust-group) across full corpus.
2. Edges: TOC hierarchy (KCL-strong), xref citation graph (KA-strong), keyword co-occurrence (small in both), embedding similarity (to fill gaps).

**Req #4 — Ingest primary content (awards, case decisions)**
**Target the `<caselaw>` schema from this repository directly.** `<juris-description>` already models parties (Applicant/Defendant/Intervener typed), court, date, case number, references — all of which are deterministically extractable from award PDFs. Then the NLP stack only has to fill `<topic>`, `<keywords>`, and the `<references>` graph. This is the cleanest Req #4 target we have in either corpus.

## 9. Schema & parsing reference (Data-Engineer view)

This section answers four specific questions a data engineer typically asks before committing to a pipeline: **(a) what is the XML hierarchy, (b) which tags are payload vs metadata, (c) what data-quality issues exist, (d) how hard is it to ship as LLM-ready JSON with semantic chunking.** See the KA findings §8 for the same structure against the KA sample — the schema is shared; the quantified issues differ.

### 9a. XML hierarchy & schema

All files: DTD `-//KLI//DTD KEA-BASIC//EN` → `keabasic.dtd` (external, not shipped with samples). Reconstructed from 21 KCL files:

```
<KEA-publ>
└── <essay | legislation | commentary | caselaw | booktoc>   ← 5 doc types
    ├── <frontmatter>                                        ← METADATA (see 9b)
    │   ├── <title>
    │   ├── <authorgroup><author><name>First<surname value="123">Last</surname></name></author>…
    │   └── <publication-info publication-type="book|loose-leaf|journal">
    │       ├── <title>, <short-title/>, <publ-cluster/>
    │       ├── <authorgroup><editor>…</editor></authorgroup>
    │       ├── <edition><volume/><issue/><supplement/></edition>
    │       ├── <publ-date value="YYYYMMDD"/>
    │       ├── <publisher id="publ-020"><name>…</name></publisher>
    │       ├── <isbn/> <issn/>
    │       ├── <range first="…" last="…" type="pp"/>
    │       ├── <cust-group value="KCL|CH"/>                  # may repeat
    │       └── <copyright>
    │
    ├── <text-description>                                   ← EXISTING ENRICHMENT
    │   ├── <topic id="KCL-004">Liberalisation</topic>             # see 9b — ID-coded
    │   ├── <keywords><keyword>…</keyword>…</keywords>             # rare in KCL
    │   └── <text-scope>
    │       ├── <country value="IN">India</country>
    │       └── <organization/>
    │
    └── <text | legis-text | juris-comment | legis-comment | juris>   ← PAYLOAD (varies by type)
        ├── <abstract/>                                      # rare in KCL (2/21)
        └── <section id="a00001">                            # nests up to depth 4 in KCL
            ├── <number/> <title/>
            ├── <p>… inline markup, <xref/>, <note/>, <list/>, <blockquote/> …</p>
            └── <section>…</section>                         # recursive
```

**Doc-type-specific body containers — the same five shapes KA has, plus `<caselaw>`:**

| Doc type | Body wrapper | Body element | Count in KCL |
|---|---|---|---|
| `essay` | `<text>` | `<section>` | 13 |
| `legislation` | `<legis>` → `<legis-text>` | `<legis-section>` | 3 |
| **`caselaw`** (KCL-only — not in KA) | `<juris>` → `<juris-text>` | `<section>` + `<juris-description>` with `<juris-authority>`, `<juris-number>`, `<juris-date>`, `<juris-parties>`, `<references>` | 2 |
| `commentary` (legis-note) | `<legis-comment>` | `<section>` + `<legis-description>` | 2 |
| `booktoc` | `<booktoc-text>` | `<booktoc-section>` (navigation only) | 1 |

### 9b. Payload vs metadata tag classification

Five tag classes, each handled differently by the pipeline:

| Class | Tags | Role | How pipeline treats them |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Structural identity** | `<KEA-publ>`, `<essay>`, `<legislation>`, `<commentary>`, `<caselaw>`, `<booktoc>` (+ `id`, `lang`, `essay-type`, `commentary-type`, `juris-type` attrs) | Routing / schema dispatch | Selects parse path; emitted as top-level JSON `doc_type`. |
| **Bibliographic metadata** | `<frontmatter>`, `<title>`, `<authorgroup>`, `<publication-info>`, `<publ-date>`, `<publisher>`, `<isbn>`, `<issn>`, `<edition>`, `<range>`, `<cust-group>`, `<copyright>`, `<updated>` | Catalog facets, filters, access control | Flat JSON keys. Never in LLM chunk text. |
| **Existing enrichment (metadata)** | `<text-description>`, `<topic id="KCL-XXX">`, `<keywords>/<keyword>`, `<text-scope>/<country>`, `<text-scope>/<organization>` | Retrieval tags + seed taxonomy | Flat JSON lists. In KCL, `topic/@id` is the **canonical key** — prefer it over the English text, which is display-layer. |
| **Case-specific metadata** (caselaw + commentary) | `<juris-description>`, `<juris-parties>/<juris-party>`, `<juris-authority>` / `<juris-court>`, `<juris-date>`, `<juris-name>`, `<juris-number>`, `<references>` | Structured case facets | Separate JSON block exposing deterministic retrieval facets (court, date range, party role). |
| **PRIMARY TEXT PAYLOAD** | `<text>`, `<legis-text>`, `<juris-text>`, `<juris-comment>`, `<legis-comment>` + descendants: `<section>`/`<legis-section>`, `<p>`, `<abstract>`, `<blockquote>`, `<list>/<item>`, inline `<it>`, `<bd>`, `<sc>`, `<number>` | **The content** | Recursively flattened to plain text; inline markup dropped; `<xref>` captured separately as graph edges; `<note>` footnotes captured but NOT inlined into section text. |

**Same straddler note as KA:** `<xref>` is structurally inside payload but semantically metadata — emit to `doc.xrefs`, drop from flattened text.

### 9c. Data-quality issues (measured against KCL sample)

| # | Issue | Quantified in KCL sample | Impact | Pipeline response |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Commented-out legacy `<frontmatter>`** kept alongside current one | **18/21 files (86%)** — higher than KA's 56% | Naïve XPath returns duplicates | Strip XML comments before parsing |
| **2** | **Internal DOCTYPE entity subsets** declaring binary GIF figures | **5/21 files (24%)** — the Roy chapters with figures | Default stdlib ET rejects the files | Strip DOCTYPE + internal subset before parse |
| **3** | **External DTD reference** not shipped with samples | All 21 files | Blocks validating parsers; schema must be inferred | Non-validating parse; reconstruct schema from data |
| **4** | **Keyword vocabulary near-empty** | Only 6 unique keywords total (1 caselaw doc has them; everyone else, 0) | Req #3 keyword dictionary cannot seed from KCL alone | Combine with KA keyword vocab; use KA as seed for keyword extraction in KCL |
| **5** | **Empty-body placeholders** | **1/21 files** — `TOC-Roy-2024.xml` (TOC is empty by design) | Chunker must filter, not fail | Parser emits `word_count == 0`; skip in chunking |
| **6** | **Missing `<author>` on Roy book chapters + annexes** | **12/21 files (57%)** — all 6 Roy chapters + 4 annexes + b001 + 2 caselaw docs lack `<author>` | Author indices and citation attribution break silently; author-based recommendation loses 57% of content | Inherit author from parent work (book → chapters) via `xref`/TOC traversal; for caselaw, treat `<juris-parties>` + `<juris-authority>` as the attribution surface |
| **7** | **`<organization>` tags absent entirely** | 0/21 | Organization facet unusable without NER | Must fill via NER (org entities likely recoverable from `<juris-authority>` and body text) |
| **8** | **Topic `id` codes without a published legend** | 5 distinct codes (`KCL-001`…`KCL-005`) — all consistent (no `id` maps to >1 text, no text to >1 `id`) | The taxonomy is clearly internal, but we don't have the full controlled list | **Ask the editorial team** for the full `KCL-NNN` legend; harvest at scale as fallback. Safe to treat `@id` as stable key |
| **9** | **`lang` attribute missing** on `TOC-Roy-2024.xml` | 1/21 files | Language-based routing can misclassify | Default to container's language when missing |
| **10** | **Section nesting up to depth 4** (deeper than KA's 3) | 1 file at depth 4, 6 files at depth 3 | Flat "top-level sections only" loses sub-structure; chunking must recurse | Recursive section walker for chunking (parser currently flattens past top level — acceptable for EDA, not for retrieval) |
| **11** | **Page markers as processing instructions** (`<?page nr="…"?>`) | Every paginated doc | ET exposes PIs separately; naïve text-join can duplicate whitespace | Skip PIs during flattening (parser handles this) |
| **12** | **One 1976 doc among 2024-dominant corpus** | `KLI-KCL-CL-CompL200500292.xml` | Time-based filters might exclude old-but-canonical case law | Use `publ-date` deliberately, not as a freshness filter |
| **13** | **Very wide doc-size variance (107–49,643 words)** | All 20 non-empty files; p25=1,472 / p75=24,034 | Fixed chunk size fails both tails | Adaptive chunking policy (see 9d) |

**KCL has notably fewer author-keyword issues than KA (no casing dupes — because there are almost no keywords to collide).** The topic taxonomy is the cleanest artefact in either sample: 5 distinct IDs, zero collisions.

### 9d. Ease of parsing to LLM-ready JSON + chunking plan

**Verdict: Low–medium complexity, same as KA, once the `<caselaw>` path is added.** All 21 KCL files parse with the existing stdlib parser. The single structural addition vs KA was handling the `<caselaw>` → `<juris>` → `<juris-description>` + `<juris-text>` path (done; see §4 and the parser at `src/ka_parser/parser.py`).

**Target JSON shape (what `parsed_docs.json` emits today):**

```json
{
  "doc_id": "KLI-KCL-0949015",
  "doc_type": "caselaw",
  "essay_type": null,
  "lang": "en",
  "metadata": {
    "title": "Commission v. Belgium, Judgment of the Court of Justice of 3 December 2009, Case C-475/08",
    "authors": [],
    "editors": [],
    "container_title": "...",
    "publisher": "...",
    "publ_year": 2009, "publ_date": "20091203",
    "isbn": "...", "issn": null,
    "cust_groups": ["KCL"]
  },
  "enrichment": {
    "topics": [{"id": "KCL-004", "text": "Liberalisation"}],   // ID-coded for KCL
    "keywords": ["Failure of a Member State to fulfil obligations", "Directive 2003/55/EC", ...],
    "countries": [], "organizations": []
  },
  "case_metadata": {                                            // populated for caselaw + commentary
    "court": "European Union Court of Justice of the European Communities (ECJ)",
    "case_number": "C-475/08",
    "case_name": null,
    "decision_date": "20091203",
    "parties": [
      {"role": "Applicant", "name": "Commission"},
      {"role": "Defendant", "name": "Belgium"}
    ]
  },
  "body": {
    "word_count": 4090,
    "sections": [
      {"id": "a00001", "number": null, "title": "...", "text": "...", "word_count": 800},
      ...
    ],
    "plain_text": "..."
  },
  "edges": {
    "xrefs": [{"target": "...", "type": "ref|toc", "text": "..."}],
    "references": []                                           // populate from <references> at scale
  }

```

**Semantic chunking recommendation (tuned to KCL's measured distribution):**

KCL top-level sections range **4 to 27,206 words** (median 1,105, p75 3,653). The variance is wider than KA — book chapters dominate the upper tail. Recommended policy (same shape as KA, re-tuned):

| Scenario | Chunk strategy |
|---|---|
| Doc ≤ 500 words (annexes, EU-notice snippets) | **Whole doc = 1 chunk.** 25% of KCL fits here — don't waste splitter cycles |
| Section ≤ 1,000 words | **Whole section = 1 chunk.** Carry breadcrumbs: `doc_id`, `section.id`, `section.title`, `doc.metadata`, `doc.enrichment.topics (by id)`, `case_metadata (if caselaw)` |
| Section 1,000–4,000 words | **Split on paragraph boundaries**, target ~600 words/chunk with 60-word overlap |
| Section > 4,000 words (Roy chapters) | **Recursive nested-section split first** (depth ≤ 4 in KCL), then paragraph split. Chapter-level summaries from `<section title>` paths make excellent embedding-time metadata |
| Any size | Emit `<abstract>` as dedicated chunk (`chunk_type: "abstract"`) when present — rare in KCL (2/21), but very high value when available |
| Caselaw | **Case metadata is a dedicated chunk** (`chunk_type: "case_header"`) — small, high-precision for "find me the Coca-Cola merger case" type queries, independent of body matching |
| Footnotes | Extract `<note>` subtrees to sibling list; do NOT inline into section text |

**Per-chunk metadata to attach:**
```
doc_id, doc_type, chunk_id, chunk_type, section_path, section_title,
publ_year, container_title, cust_groups, lang,
topics_by_id,    // ["KCL-004"] — stable retrieval key
topics_text,     // ["Liberalisation"] — display
countries, keywords,
case_metadata    // court, case_number, decision_date, parties (caselaw/commentary only)
```

**KCL-specific landmines** (in addition to the 5 KA landmines):

1. **Caselaw is a 3rd body-wrapper shape** (`<juris>` → `<juris-text>`). A pipeline built only for KA would silently drop all caselaw bodies (2/21 = 10% of KCL).
2. **`<references>` container inside `<juris-description>`** holds citation targets that are richer than inline `<xref>` — currently the parser flags the element but doesn't recurse; worth adding at full-corpus scale (Req #3 citation graph).
3. **Topic `id` vs text mismatch risk**: embed by `@id`, display by text. Treating the English text as the key will silently break when the same topic is rendered differently in a future issue.
4. **Book chapters without authors** (57% of KCL) need **author inheritance from the parent book** — otherwise author-based retrieval loses the majority of KCL content.
5. **DOCTYPE internal-entity subsets are ~5× more common in KCL** (24%) than in KA (4%) because KCL book chapters have many figures. This single parser bug fix unlocks 5 files in KCL vs 1 in KA.

## 10. Recommended next EDA passes
